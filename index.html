Introduction

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist. Nikola Tesla is regarded as one of the most brilliant inventors in history.

His work provided the basis for the modern alternating current power system, as well as having developed both radio and the fluorescent light bulb. He worked with Thomas Edison and George Westinghouse, among others. He is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Early Life

Nikola Tesla was born an ethnic Serb in the village of Smiljan, within the Military Frontier, in the Austrian Empire (present-day Croatia), on 10 July 1856. From his childhood he showed remarkable excellence by creating and inventing things.

Tesla's interest in electricity was sparked by his mother, who invented small household appliances.

Moment of Attention

Tesla gained public attention in the 1890s when he demonstrated wireless transmission of electricity and exhibited his work on high voltage, high frequency power experiments in New York and Colorado Springs.

His experiments with high voltage and high frequency electricity paved the way for modern wireless communications.

Awards and Achievements

  • Elliott Cresson Medal (1893)
  • IEEE Edison Medal (1917)
  • Order of St. Sava (1892)
  • Order of the Yugoslav Crown (1931)
Tesla's contributions to electrical engineering and electromagnetism remain foundational in modern technology.
